PowerWash Simulator 2 features a variety of cleaning jobs, ranging from small vehicles to massive industrial complexes. As you wash away layers of dirt, grime, and moss, the game automatically saves your progress in real-time. This ensures that you do not lose your hard work if you need to exit the game mid-job. On the other hand, wiping your save data resets everything, requiring you to build your cleaning empire from scratch.

<Section icon={Target} title="PowerWash Simulator 2: How to Restart a Level Step-by-Step">

Restarting a level is a common practice when players want to optimize their cleaning routes, achieve specific achievements, or resolve a bug where dirt becomes unreachable. The individual level reset tool is designed to wipe the dirt progress of the active map without penalizing your career standing or removing your hard-earned cash.

Before initiating a reset, make sure you have claimed any pending rewards or bonuses. Once you confirm the reset, the level will reload, and all surfaces will be covered in dirt once more.   <Step num="2" title="Navigate to Job Details">
    Locate the active job panel on the tablet interface, which shows your current completion percentage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Restart Option">
    Click the reset button next to the job details to clear the dirt data for the current level.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm the Reset">
    Agree to the confirmation prompt to reload the level with 0% progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Troubleshooting Common Restart Issues">

While the reset mechanics in PowerWash Simulator 2 are generally reliable, players may occasionally run into issues where a level does not reload correctly or progress tracking becomes desynchronized. If you find that your restart button is unresponsive, or if the level loads but immediately marks objects as clean, you may need to verify your game files or check your network connection if playing in co-op mode. Multiplayer hosts have sole control over the session save state, so guests cannot independently restart levels during a shared job.
