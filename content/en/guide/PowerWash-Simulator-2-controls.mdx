<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Nozzle & Cleaning Mechanics">

Choosing the correct nozzle is just as important as knowing how to move. The cleaning physics in the game react dynamically to the angle of your spray, the surface material, and the type of grime you are tackling. If your hose pressure is too low or you use an incorrect angle, the water will smear the dirt across the surface instead of blasting it away.

Nozzle rotation is a critical mechanic. By rotating your nozzle 90 degrees, you can change your spray from a horizontal fan to a vertical fan. This allows you to match the shape of the object you are cleaning—such as thin fence posts or wide brick walls.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Control Customization & Motion Sickness Solutions">

First-person simulation games can occasionally cause motion sickness due to repetitive sweeping motions and camera bobbing. To combat this, the developers have included a dedicated "Camera Lock" feature within the **PowerWash Simulator 2 controls** settings. This feature allows you to freeze the camera in a fixed forward position, shifting the analog stick control entirely to the washer nozzle itself.

If you find yourself feeling dizzy after cleaning a large playground or a multi-story building, follow these steps to optimize your control settings for maximum comfort.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Camera Lock Mode">
    Press the Right Stick click (R3) to lock your camera in place. This detaches your nozzle aim from the screen movement, allowing you to paint the surface with water without rotating the entire camera.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Controller Sensitivity">
    Navigate to the Options Menu and lower your horizontal and vertical analog sensitivity to between 40% and 50%. This prevents erratic, high-speed camera movements when releasing the stick.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Toggle Aim Assist Settings">
    Turn on the subtle Aim Assist feature. This option helps the nozzle snap slightly to small, hard-to-see dirt spots, reducing the need for micro-adjustments with the analog stick.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Setting Option | Recommended Value | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Camera Lock Mode** | Toggle (On) | Eliminates screen sway and reduces motion sickness |
| **Analog Sensitivity** | 45% Horizontal / 40% Vertical | Provides smooth, deliberate sweeping motions |
| **Aim Assist Strength** | Medium | Assists in targeting tiny, stubborn spots of grime |
| **Show Dirt Mode** | Toggle (Instead of Hold) | Allows continuous dirt highlighting without hand strain |

<Callout type="info" title="Accessibility Focus">
Using the "Toggle" setting for the "Show Dirt" function is highly recommended. By default, you must hold the button down, which can cause finger fatigue during long play sessions. Changing this to a toggle allows you to scan for dirt with a single tap.
</Callout>
