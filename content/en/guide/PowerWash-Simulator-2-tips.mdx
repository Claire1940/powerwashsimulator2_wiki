<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering Nozzle Physics and Spray Patterns">

Understanding the physics of your equipment is the foundation of any professional cleaning career. In PowerWash Simulator 2, the game utilizes a color-coded system to help you identify the pressure and spread of your spray. Choosing the right nozzle isn't just about preference; it is about the structural integrity of the material you are cleaning and the density of the grime.

| Nozzle Color | Spray Angle | Pressure Level | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Red** | 0 Degrees | **Extreme** | Deep rust, stubborn bird droppings, tiny crevices |
| **Yellow** | 15 Degrees | **High** | Stripping old paint, cleaning concrete driveways |
| **Green** | 25 Degrees | **Medium** | General house siding, wooden decks, vehicles |
| **White** | 40 Degrees | **Low** | Rinsing large windows, light dust on plastic |
| **Soap** | Wide Fan | **Very Low** | Applying chemical cleaners to large surfaces |

The red nozzle provides a concentrated point of water that can strip almost anything, but its tiny coverage area makes it inefficient for large walls. Conversely, the white nozzle allows for rapid rinsing but will fail to remove "baked-on" algae or rust. Professional players often cycle through these rapidly using the LB/RB buttons or the selection wheel to match the dirt type instantly.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip: Nozzle Rotation">
You can rotate your nozzle vertically or horizontally by pressing the Left Trigger. This is crucial for cleaning thin objects like fence pickets or railings. Rotating the spray to match the orientation of the object ensures maximum water contact and reduces waste.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Chemical Cleaning and Soap Efficiency">

One of the most overlooked **PowerWash Simulator 2 tips** is the strategic use of cleaning liquids. While the standard water spray is free, liquids cost money. However, the time saved by using a soap dispenser on a massive stone monument often outweighs the cost of the refill. You must match the liquid type to the material indicated in the top-left corner of your HUD.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Material">
    Hover your reticle over the surface. The HUD will display "Metal," "Wood," "Stone," or "Plastic."
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip Soap Nozzle">
    Switch to the specialized soap dispenser nozzle. Ensure you have the correct liquid loaded in your inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply Evenly">
    Spray the surface until the dirt begins to "soften." You don't need to finish the job with soap; just loosen the grime.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Rinse and Finish">
    Switch back to a standard high-pressure nozzle (Green or Yellow) to blast away the loosened residue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Liquid Type | Target Material | Effectiveness | Cost per Refill |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Multi-Purpose** | Any Surface | **Medium** | $10 |
| **Metal Cleaner** | Iron, Steel, Chrome | **High** | $15 |

<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Workflow Tactics">

To become a true expert, you need to optimize your movement and camera settings. Many players experience fatigue or motion sickness due to the constant "swiping" motion required to clean large floors. PowerWash Simulator 2 includes several accessibility features that double as efficiency tools.

**Key Efficiency Features:**
- **Aim Mode (Up D-pad):** Fixes your feet in place and allows you to move only the nozzle. This is perfect for detailed work or "painting" clean lines without moving your entire field of view.
- **Auto-Spray (Left D-pad):** Toggles the water on permanently. This prevents finger strain during massive jobs like the Subway Station or the Desert Rover.
- **Dirt Highlight (Right D-pad):** Causes all remaining dirt to glow bright orange. Use this frequently to avoid missing tiny specs that prevent a 100% completion rating.

| Feature | Input (Controller) | Input (PC) | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Toggle Spray** | Left D-Pad | **C** | Reduces finger fatigue |
| **Show Dirt** | Right D-Pad | **Tab** | Locates hidden grime |
| **Aim Mode** | Up D-Pad | **V** | Precision control |
| **Change Nozzle** | LB / RB | **Scroll** | Rapid pressure adjustment |

<Callout type="info" title="Ladder Safety">
Always move your ladder or stool as you progress. Trying to reach "just a bit further" while on a ladder often results in an awkward angle where your spray loses pressure. If the water isn't removing dirt, you are likely too far away.
</Callout>
