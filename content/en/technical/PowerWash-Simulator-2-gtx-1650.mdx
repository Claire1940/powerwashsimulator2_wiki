<Section icon={Settings} title="Optimized Graphics Settings for GTX 1650">

To achieve a consistent 60 FPS in both single-player campaigns and hectic online co-op sessions, you must adjust specific graphics parameters. The game's default "High" preset can cause frame rates to dip into the low 40s during heavy cleaning phases, especially when using the soapy foam spray or washing highly reflective metallic surfaces.

By fine-tuning shader quality, shadow maps, and water simulation details, you can reclaim valuable GPU headroom. The "Soap 2.0" mechanic introduces dynamic foam that clings to dirty objects, which increases shader calculations. Lowering the water simulation quality slightly reduces the physical displacement detail of the spray without affecting your cleaning accuracy or speed.

| Setting | Default Value | Recommended Value | Performance Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Water Simulation** | High | **Medium** | High (5-8% FPS gain) |
| **Shadow Quality** | Ultra | **Medium** | High (4-6% FPS gain) |
| **Ambient Occlusion** | High | **Low** | Medium (3-4% FPS gain) |
| **Anti-Aliasing** | TAA | **FXAA** | Medium (3-5% FPS gain) |
| **Texture Resolution** | High | **High** | Low (VRAM dependent) |
| **Dirt Highlight Glow** | On | **On** | Very Low (<1% impact) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Optimization Tip">
Keep Texture Resolution set to **High** if you have 4 GB of VRAM. Reducing it to Medium makes the dirt textures look blurry, which makes finding the final 1% of grime much harder. Instead, save performance by disabling demanding anti-aliasing methods.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sliders} title="Step-by-Step Optimization Guide">

Follow these steps to configure your operating system and graphics control panel for the best performance. These adjustments ensure your hardware allocates maximum power to the game while eliminating background interruptions.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Configure Nvidia Control Panel Settings">
    Right-click your desktop and open the Nvidia Control Panel. Navigate to Manage 3D Settings, select the game's executable, and set Power Management Mode to Prefer Maximum Performance. Change Texture Filtering Quality to Performance to gain a steady 2-3 FPS boost.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable Windows Hardware-Accelerated GPU Scheduling">
    Open the Windows Settings app, go to System, then Display, and select Graphics Settings. Toggle the switch for Hardware-Accelerated GPU Scheduling (HAGS) to the On position. This reduces CPU overhead and improves frame consistency on mid-range cards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Set a Custom Frame Rate Limit">
    In the game's display options, enable V-Sync or set a custom frame rate cap of 60 FPS. Capping your frame rate prevents the GPU from running at 100% load during simple menu screens, lowering overall power draw and keeping system temperatures stable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Update Your Nvidia Game Ready Drivers">
    Ensure you have the latest 2026 Nvidia drivers installed via GeForce Experience or the official website. Driver updates often contain specific engine optimizations and bug fixes for newly released DLC packs and seasonal updates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Laptop Users">
Ensure Windows Power Mode is set to High Performance. If your laptop features a dual-GPU setup (integrated Intel/AMD graphics alongside the GTX 1650), verify in the Nvidia Control Panel that the high-performance Nvidia processor is selected as the preferred graphics processor.
</Callout>

</Section>
