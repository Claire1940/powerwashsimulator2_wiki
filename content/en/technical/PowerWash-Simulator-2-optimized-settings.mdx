<Section icon={Target} title="Input and Control Configurations">

Handling a high-pressure washer for hours can be taxing. The sequel introduces several quality-of-life "settings" that were highly requested by the community. Adjusting your input sensitivity and button mapping can significantly reduce the time spent on a single vehicle or building.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Toggle Wash">
    Navigate to the Options menu and find the "Wash Mode" setting. Change this from "Hold" to "Toggle." This allows you to spray continuously without holding down the trigger or mouse button, which is vital for the 38-job Career campaign.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Aim Sensitivity">
    PowerWash Simulator 2 requires both broad sweeps and pixel-perfect precision. Set your "General Sensitivity" to a higher value for large panels, but keep "Precision Aim Sensitivity" lower for cleaning seams, bolts, and small trim.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Optimize Field of View (FOV)">
    Increase your FOV to at least 90. A wider field of view helps you track your cleaning progress across large structures like the Airship or the Temple Interior and significantly reduces the "tunnel vision" effect that causes motion sickness.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure the Nozzle Wheel">
    Ensure your nozzle rotation and selection keys are bound to accessible buttons. In the heat of the Star Wars Pack missions, switching from a 25° nozzle to the 0° red nozzle quickly is key to efficiency.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Controller Setup">
For console players, mapping "Dirt Highlight" to a paddle or a comfortable shoulder button is highly recommended. You will be pulsing this button thousands of times to find the last specks of dirt.
</Callout>

</Section>
