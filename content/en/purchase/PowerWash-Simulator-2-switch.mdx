<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Controls on Switch">

One point of contention for veterans is the control scheme. Surprisingly, **PowerWash Simulator 2 switch** does not feature gyroscopic aiming or a "mouse mode" for the touch screen at launch. This makes precision cleaning with the analog sticks slightly more challenging. However, the developers have included several "Controller Ease" features to compensate.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Camera Lock">
    Navigate to the options menu and toggle **Camera Lock**. This allows you to move the nozzle independently with the right stick while the camera stays fixed, significantly reducing motion sickness.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Nozzle Rotation">
    Use the **D-pad** to rotate your nozzle on the fly. Switching between horizontal and vertical spray patterns is essential for following the grain of wood or the edges of brickwork.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Stance Changes">
    The sequel introduces more fluid movement. Don't just stand; use the **Crouch** and **Prone** buttons to get under vehicles or behind pipes where dirt often hides.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Highlight Remaining Dirt">
    If you are stuck at 99%, use the **Dirt Highlight** button. This will pulse the remaining grime in a bright color, making those last pesky specks visible.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Control Input | Function | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Right Stick** | Aim Nozzle | Use with Camera Lock for precision. |
| **Left Stick** | Strafe/Move | Always strafe while spraying for even coverage. |
| **D-Pad Up/Down** | Change Nozzle | Red is high pressure; White is wide spray. |
| **D-Pad Left/Right** | Rotate Nozzle | Match the angle to the object's edge. |
| **L/R Triggers** | Spray/Soap | Toggle "Continuous Spray" in settings to save fingers. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Motion Sickness Warning">
Because of the lack of gyro controls, the constant "swaying" of the camera can cause nausea for some players. **Always** enable Camera Lock and increase the "Deadzone" settings to stabilize the view.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Traversal and Verticality">

The level design in the sequel is much more vertical. You are no longer tethered to the ground or simple stepladders. The introduction of pulleys, abseiling, and industrial scissor lifts allows you to tackle massive structures like clock towers and multi-story buildings.

| Traversal Tool | How to Use | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scissor Lift** | Interact with the control panel | Reaching high, flat building faces. |
| **Abseiling Gear** | Attach to designated roof hooks | Cleaning steep inclines or cliff sides. |
| **Pulleys** | Pull the rope to ascend | Quick vertical movement in tight spaces. |
| **Scaffolding** | Manual assembly in levels | Customizable height for complex shapes. |

The physics of these devices are realistic. When on a scissor lift, the platform will sway slightly, affecting your spray's accuracy. Mastering the timing of your spray while the platform moves is a key skill for late-game contracts.

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Tip">
When cleaning high areas, always start from the top and work your way down. The new water physics allow "dirty water" to run down, and while it won't re-soil a perfectly clean area, it can make it harder to see the remaining grime on the lower sections.
</Callout>
