<Section icon={Map} title="PowerWash Simulator 2 PS5 Co-op & Setup">

Playing **PowerWash Simulator 2 ps5** offers one of the most relaxing and satisfying cooperative gaming experiences on the console. The sequel expands heavily on the original multiplayer foundation, introducing shared campaign progression and local split-screen play. PlayStation 5 players can tackle the massive 38-job Career Mode entirely solo, with a local partner on the same screen, or in an online team of up to four players.

To get started with cooperative cleaning on the PlayStation 5, players must understand how save progression and platform requirements function. While local split-screen is completely free to use offline, taking your cleaning crew online requires an active subscription. Furthermore, online co-op sessions utilize host-only progression, meaning guest players contribute to the host's campaign rather than unlocking career milestones on their own profile.

| Mode | Max Players | Save Progression | Requirements |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Solo | 1 Player | Full career save progress | None |
| Local Split-Screen | 2 Players | Shared host campaign save | Second controller connected |
| Online Co-op | 4 Players | Host progression only | PlayStation Plus subscription |

Hosting or joining a session on the PlayStation 5 is straightforward. From the pause menu, the host can change the Session Type from Singleplayer to Online Multiplayer. This action generates a unique Room Code that can be shared directly with friends. Joining players simply enter this code in their multiplayer menu to instantly drop into the host's active job.

<Callout type="warning" title="PlayStation Plus Required">
Online multiplayer on the PlayStation 5 requires an active PlayStation Plus subscription. If you do not have an active subscription, you will be limited to Solo play or Local Split-Screen mode.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Equipment Tier List & Upgrades">

Succeeding in the late-game jobs requires upgrading from your light-duty starter washers to medium, heavy, and professional-grade cleaning equipment. The sequel features two primary manufacturer lines: the balanced Prime Vista series and the high-pressure Urban X series. Additionally, universal attachments like the Adaptable Nozzle and the SwirlForce Surf Ace surface cleaner completely change how you approach large-scale environments.

Choosing the right equipment depends on the surface material and the severity of the grime. While broad fan nozzles work perfectly for dust and light mud, stubborn rust, oil stains, and deep-seated moss require concentrated pressure.

| Tier | Equipment Name | Category | Best For | Why It Excels |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|

The Adaptable Nozzle stands out as the single most important upgrade to purchase early in your career. It can be fitted onto almost any washer in your inventory, allowing you to manually adjust your spray width on the fly without pausing to swap attachments.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Finding the Last Dirt & 100% Completion">

One of the most common challenges players face is getting stuck at 99% completion on a massive job, unable to locate the final speck of dirt. The sequel addresses this frustration by introducing an upgraded object checklist, multi-target marking, and highly visible orange HUD indicators that guide you directly to incomplete components.

Rather than spraying surfaces blindly, achieving 100% completion requires a systematic approach. By utilizing your tablet, tracking specific dirty components, and changing your physical perspective, you can quickly trigger the satisfying completion chime on even the largest structures.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open the Object List">
    Access your tablet menu and open the active job checklist. Sort the list to identify any named component that sits below 100% completion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mark the Unfinished Task">
    Select the incomplete item directly from the checklist. This places a bright orange marker in your HUD, pointing you toward the exact location of the object.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Pulse the Dirt Highlight">
    Approach the marked object and press the dirt highlight button. This temporarily highlights any remaining grime in a glowing yellow or orange color.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Switch to Precision Nozzles">
    Equip a narrow 15-degree nozzle, a 0-degree red nozzle, or a narrowed Adaptable Nozzle to apply maximum pressure directly to the glowing spots.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Check Hidden Angles">
    Crouch, jump, or adjust your scaffolding to inspect the undersides, interior seams, and rear faces of the component where dirt easily hides.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Listen for the Chime">
Each completed component triggers a distinct metallic chime and flashes white momentarily. If you hear this sound, the component is fully clean, and you should immediately move on to the next target.
</Callout>

</Section>
