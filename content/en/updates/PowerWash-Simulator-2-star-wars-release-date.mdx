<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Cleaning Strategy">

Tackling large-scale galactic structures requires a systematic approach. Working randomly leads to missed spots and wasted time. Follow this structured process to maximize your efficiency.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Cleaning Tip">
Always start from the highest point of the vehicle or structure and work your way down to prevent dirty water from dripping onto already cleaned areas.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scaffolding Setup">
    Position your ladders and scaffolding around the vehicle. For larger ships like the Millennium Falcon, establishing high-altitude platforms is crucial for reaching the upper hull.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Debris Pre-Wash">
    Use the low-pressure soap nozzle with the specialized galactic cleaning fluid to loosen thick layers of space dust and carbon residue.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Detailed Pressure Cleaning">
    Switch to the 15-degree or 25-degree nozzle. Clean individual panels systematically, focusing on the seams and rivets where dirt accumulates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Inspection and Detailing">
    Use the dirt finder utility to highlight remaining spots. Use the 0-degree nozzle for stubborn rust or grease in hard-to-reach mechanical joints.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Nozzle Type | Best Use Case | Efficiency Rating | Damage Risk |
| 0-Degree (Red) | **Stubborn rust, engine grease** | Excellent | High |
| 15-Degree (Yellow) | **Carbon scoring, metal panels** | Very Good | Medium |
| 25-Degree (Green) | **General dust, ship hulls** | Good | Low |
| Soap Nozzle (Black) | **Pre-wash soaking, organic stains** | Utility | None |
