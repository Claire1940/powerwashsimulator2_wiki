The **PowerWash Simulator 2 inside shooting** gallery is one of the most visually distinct early-game assignments in the Career Mode. Located at the Wengerloof Carnival in the Muckingham Recreation Ground, this job tasks players with restoring a grime-covered attraction to its former glory. Because many of these parts are small and recessed, choosing the right pressure setting is critical to avoid wasting water and time.

<Section icon={Target} title="Precision Equipment for Inside Shooting Jobs">

When tackling the **PowerWash Simulator 2 inside shooting** gallery, your choice of nozzle determines how quickly you can clear the fine details. The targets are significantly smaller than the panels on the Removals Van or the walls of the Public Facility. High-pressure, narrow-angle nozzles are your best friends for the interior mechanisms, while wider fans are better for the large backboard and floor.

The following table compares the efficiency of various nozzles specifically for the Shooting Gallery's unique geometry:

| Nozzle Type | Cleaning Width | Pressure Level | Best Used For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0° Red Nozzle** | Very Narrow | **Maximum** | Stubborn rust on target hinges and deep seams |
| **15° Yellow Nozzle** | Narrow | High | Individual targets (ducks, stars) and lettering |
| **25° Green Nozzle** | Medium | Moderate | Main wooden backboard and side panels |
| **40° White Nozzle** | Wide | Low | Rinsing large flat floor areas and the ceiling |
| **Adaptable Nozzle** | Variable | Dynamic | Switching quickly between targets and the booth frame |
| **Soap Nozzle** | Very Wide | Low | Pre-treating the heavily soiled floor and counter |

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the Mechanisms">
The tracks that move the targets can hide significant amounts of dirt. Ensure you crouch and aim upward into the rail slots to reach the 100% completion threshold for the "Tracks" and "Supports" components.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Step-by-Step Shooting Gallery Cleaning Route">

To achieve a perfect score without hunting for the final 1% of dirt, follow this systematic route. The **PowerWash Simulator 2 inside shooting** gallery is best cleaned from the inside out and top to bottom. This ensures that dirty water from the ceiling doesn't ruin your freshly cleaned floor.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Bigfoot Priority">
    Before cleaning anything else, locate the **Bigfoot Target** at the back of the gallery. Focusing solely on this target until it pings 100% will unlock the **Where's Woolly?** achievement. Avoid using wide nozzles during this step to prevent accidental cleaning of adjacent targets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Ceiling and Upper Frame">
    Stand inside the booth and look up. Clean the ceiling panels and the upper support beams first. Use the 25° nozzle for the broad panels and the 15° nozzle for the corners where the beams meet the walls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Backboard and Side Walls">
    Sweep the large vertical surfaces. These areas hold the most visible grime. Start from the top left and move in horizontal lines across to the right. The 25° nozzle provides the best balance of speed and pressure for these wooden surfaces.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Target Rows and Tracks">
    This is the most time-consuming part. Clean each row of targets (Ducks, Stars, and Pipes) individually. Make sure to spray the front, back, and the narrow edges of every target. Use the 15° nozzle to ensure you hit the small hinges that connect them to the tracks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Counter and Floor">
    Finish the job by cleaning the front counter where the "rifles" would sit, followed by the floor. Use the **Soap Nozzle** if the floor grime is particularly thick, then rinse it away with the 40° nozzle or the **SwirlForce Surf Ace** if you have unlocked it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Achievement Unlocked">
If you successfully cleaned the Bigfoot target first, you should see the "Where's Woolly?" notification. If you accidentally cleaned another part to 100% first, you will need to replay the job in Free Play to earn this achievement.
</Callout>

</Section>
