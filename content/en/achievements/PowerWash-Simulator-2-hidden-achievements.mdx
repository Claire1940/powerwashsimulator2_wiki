<Section icon={Trophy} title="Complex Environmental Puzzles">

Some **PowerWash Simulator 2 hidden achievements** require multiple steps or precise movement. The "Tea Party" and "Floor is Lava" achievements are prime examples of tasks that test your patience and platforming skills within the simulator's physics engine.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Tea Party Setup">
    In the Teapot level, locate all six hidden gnomes scattered across the map. One is even located on top of the teapot itself.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Seating the Guests">
    Carefully place each gnome into one of the chairs. This was historically buggy, so ensure they are seated firmly and upright to trigger the 'Teton' achievement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rise and Shine">
    Take one of those gnomes onto the lift with you. Raise the lift to its maximum height to unlock this secondary secret.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Pillar Jump">
    In the Stone Circle level, start on the platform and jump between the stone pillars. You must complete the full circle without touching the grass.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The "Floor is Lava" challenge can be frustrating due to the game's jumping mechanics. It is often helpful to crouch-jump to gain a bit more control over your landing. If you touch the ground, you don't need to restart the level—just climb back to the starting platform and try the sequence again.

<Callout type="success" title="Physics Patch">
Recent 2026 updates have improved the 'snap' feature for placing gnomes in chairs, making the Tea Party achievement much more reliable than in previous versions.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Solar System and Late-Game Mastery">

The Solar System level is widely considered the most difficult for completionists. It requires a strict adherence to planetary order and a deep dive into the Earth's internal structure. Missing even one small part of the Earth's core can invalidate the entire run.

| Planet Sequence | Target | Special Instructions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | The Sun | Must be cleaned 100% first |
| 2 | Mercury | Clean fully after the Sun |
| 3 | Venus | Clean fully after Mercury |
| 4 | Earth | Clean all layers: Crust, Inner Core, Mantle, Outer Core |
| 5 | Mars & Beyond | Follow the standard orbital distance order |

When cleaning Earth, use the job breakdown menu frequently. You must ensure the Earth's Crust, Inner Core, Mantle, Outer Core, and Super Plume are all removed from the list before moving to the next planet. The Moon, Moon Arm, and Pedestal do not count toward the "Earth" portion of the achievement and should be ignored until later.

<Callout type="info" title="Final Level Strategy">
In the Town Float level, the 'Squeaky Clean' achievement requires you to clean the mouse last. This includes the body, head, ears, and tail. Use a small, precise nozzle to avoid accidental overspray on these parts while cleaning the rest of the float.
</Callout>

</Section>
