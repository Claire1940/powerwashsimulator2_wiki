<Section icon={Target} title="Nozzles and Extensions: Adjusting Your Spray Area">

Nozzles and extensions are the primary attachments that modify your spray pattern, water pressure, and reach. Using the wrong nozzle can result in wasted time or incomplete cleaning on stubborn surfaces. Standard color-coded nozzles offer fixed angles, while specialized attachments introduce unique mechanics.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Fixed Nozzles">

    - **Yellow Nozzle (15-degree)**: High pressure, narrow strip for heavy grime.
    - **Green Nozzle (25-degree)**: Balanced pressure and coverage for general use.
    - **White Nozzle (40-degree)**: Low pressure, wide fan for sweeping loose dirt.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Turbo Nozzle">

    - High-pressure impact
    - Covers a larger surface area rapidly
    - Best for stubborn algae on roofs and concrete
  </Card>
  <Card title="Extensions & Adaptables">
    - **Standard Extension**: Increases reach for high walls.
    - **Extra Long Extension**: Reaches roofs without ladders.
    - **Adaptable Nozzle**: Fine-tunes pressure relative to target distance.
  </Card>

Selecting the correct nozzle depends entirely on the type of dirt and the material of the surface you are cleaning. For instance, glass and delicate wood trims can easily be cleared with a wide white nozzle, whereas stone foundations and metal frames require the concentrated force of a green or yellow nozzle.

| Nozzle Type | Spray Angle | Grime Penetration | Coverage Speed | Recommended Surfaces |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Red Nozzle** | 0 Degrees | **Excellent** | Very Slow | Metal joints, deep cracks, rust |
| **Yellow Nozzle** | 15 Degrees | **Very Good** | Slow | Concrete, brickwork, stone |
| **Green Nozzle** | 25 Degrees | **Good** | Medium | Painted walls, wood paneling, plastics |
| **White Nozzle** | 40 Degrees | **Fair** | Fast | Glass windows, solar panels, vehicles |
| **Turbo Nozzle** | Rotating | **Excellent** | Fast | Roof tiles, large patios, stone floors |

<Callout type="tip" title="Nozzle Efficiency">
The Turbo Nozzle is the most versatile tool in your arsenal. It combines the deep-cleaning power of a narrow 0-degree beam with the coverage speed of a wider nozzle by spinning the water stream rapidly. Use it as your default nozzle for large concrete or stone surfaces.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Grime Types and Soap Selection Mechanics">

Certain types of grime in PowerWash Simulator 2 cannot be easily removed with pure water pressure, even when using the strongest washers. Heavy staining, thick black algae, and industrial grease require chemical assistance. This is where specialized cleaning liquids and the soap nozzle become essential parts of your **PowerWash Simulator 2 equipment** loadout.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Grime and Surface Material">
    Inspect the object to determine if it is wood, metal, stone, or glass. Applying the wrong soap type will result in wasted chemicals and minimal cleaning progress.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip the Soap Nozzle and Select Liquid">
    Attach the specialized Soap Nozzle to your current power washer. Open your inventory wheel to select the corresponding cleaning liquid for the target material.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Apply a Light Coat of Soap">
    Spray the soapy mixture over the dirty surface. Watch for the grime to dissolve into bubbles. Do not over-saturate, as soap is a consumable resource that costs money.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Switch to High-Pressure Water to Rinse">
    Swap back to a standard nozzle (such as the green or yellow nozzle) and wash away the loosened grime and soap residue to achieve a perfect, clean finish.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Using the correct chemical agent saves both time and water. The table below details the different cleaning liquids, their target surfaces, and how effective they are against specific grime types.

| Cleaning Liquid | Target Material | Primary Grime Type | Chemical Cost | Efficiency Rating |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wood Cleaner** | Redwood, Oak, Pine | Moss, mold, dirt stains | Low | **★★★★★** |
| **Metal Cleaner** | Iron, steel, copper | Rust, grease, industrial oil | Medium | **★★★★☆** |
| **Stone Cleaner** | Concrete, brick, granite | Black algae, deep soot, lichen | Medium | **★★★★★** |
| **Glass Cleaner** | Windows, mirrors, screens | Water spots, dust film, fingerprints | Low | **★★★★☆** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Manage Your Chemical Budget">
Soaps are consumable items that must be purchased from the shop. Overusing them on simple dirt will quickly drain your career funds. Only use soaps on stubborn, dark-colored stains or high-altitude areas that are difficult to reach with standard high-pressure streams.
</Callout>
