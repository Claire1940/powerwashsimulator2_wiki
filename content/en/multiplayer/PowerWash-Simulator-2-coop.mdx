<Section icon={Target} title="How to Host and Join Cooperative Sessions">

Setting up a cooperative session in PowerWash Simulator 2 is straightforward, but it requires navigating the in-game multiplayer menu. The game utilizes a room code system to facilitate connections across different storefronts on PC, ensuring that Steam, Epic Games Store, and Windows Store players can connect seamlessly.

Follow these steps to host or join an active cleaning crew:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Multiplayer Menu">
    Launch PowerWash Simulator 2 and open the pause menu or navigate to the main dashboard. Select the Multiplayer option to open the connection settings.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Your Session Type">
    If you are hosting, change your Session Type from Singleplayer to Online Multiplayer. This will generate a unique, temporary Room Code on your screen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Share the Connection Code">
    Copy the generated Room Code and send it to your friends via Discord, text, or your platform's messaging system. 
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Join an Active Room">
    For joining players, select Enter a Code in the multiplayer menu, paste the host's code, and confirm to instantly connect to the session.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Use Platform Invites">
If you are playing on the same platform ecosystem (such as Steam-to-Steam or Xbox-to-Xbox), you can bypass the room code system entirely by using the standard platform overlay to send direct invites.
</Callout>

For local split-screen, the process is even simpler. Connect a second controller to your console or PC, open the multiplayer menu, and select the Split-Screen option. The screen will divide vertically or horizontally depending on your settings, allowing both players to contribute to the same active campaign save.

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Crossplay and Platform Requirements">

Cross-platform multiplayer is a highly requested feature, but it comes with specific limitations in PowerWash Simulator 2. The game supports crossplay across the Xbox and PC ecosystems, but console-specific restrictions apply to PlayStation and Nintendo users.

The PC player pool is completely unified. If you purchased the game on Steam, you can play with friends who own it on the Epic Games Store or the Windows Store. Additionally, Xbox Series X|S players can join these PC sessions seamlessly. However, PlayStation 5 and Nintendo Switch 2 players are restricted to their own platform-specific matchmaking pools.

| Platform | Crossplay Partners | Save Transfer | Subscription Required |
| Xbox Series X/S | PC (Steam, Epic, MS Store) | No | Xbox Game Pass |
| PC (Steam/Epic) | Xbox Series X/S, PC Platforms | No | None |
| PlayStation 5 | PlayStation Pool Only | No | PlayStation Plus |
| Nintendo Switch 2 | Switch 2 Pool Only | No | Nintendo Switch Online |

<Callout type="warning" title="Online Subscription Warning">
Console players must have an active online subscription to play online co-op. This includes PlayStation Plus for PS5, Nintendo Switch Online for Switch 2, and an eligible Xbox Game Pass tier for Xbox consoles.
</Callout>

It is also important to note that the game does not support cross-platform save transfers. While you can play with friends across supported platforms, your Career Mode progress, unlocked washers, and earned cash will remain tied to the platform ecosystem where the save file was originally created.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Shared Progression and Best Co-op Strategies">

To clean efficiently in cooperative play, your team should avoid clustering in the same spot. Because water pressure drops at longer ranges, having multiple players spray the exact same surface is highly inefficient. Instead, dividing the map into distinct zones or roles will dramatically speed up your completion times.

When a team joins an online Career session, all players gain access to the host's unlocked equipment and soap inventory. This allows late-game players to assist beginners by bringing high-tier washers into early-game levels, making quick work of stubborn dirt layers.
