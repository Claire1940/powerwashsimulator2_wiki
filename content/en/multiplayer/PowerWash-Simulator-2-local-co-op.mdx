<Section icon={Map} title="Setting Up PowerWash Simulator 2 Local Co Op">

Playing with a partner on the same screen is one of the most satisfying ways to tackle the grime in Muckingham. Setting up **PowerWash Simulator 2 local co op** is straightforward, but it requires proper controller synchronization and profile management depending on whether you play on PC, Xbox, PlayStation 5, or Nintendo Switch 2.

Unlike online multiplayer which requires active network subscriptions, local split-screen allows two players to clean side-by-side using a single system and two input devices.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Connect Your Controllers">
    Ensure both controllers are connected and recognized by your console or PC. On PC, you can use a combination of keyboard/mouse and a controller, or two separate gamepads. On consoles, make sure both controllers are synced to the system.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Launch the Game and Access the Menu">
    Start the game using the primary player's profile. Once on the main menu or inside the Home Base, open the pause menu and navigate to the Multiplayer settings panel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Activate Split-Screen Mode">
    Toggle the multiplayer session type from Singleplayer to Local Co-Op. The game will prompt the second player to press a specific button (typically Options, Menu, or Plus) to join the session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Select Your Job and Start Cleaning">
    Once Player 2 is successfully registered, use the Job Map in the Home Base to select any available Career or Free Play assignment. The screen will split horizontally, and both players will load into the job together.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Controller Profile Warning">
On consoles like Xbox and PlayStation 5, Player 2 should ideally sign into a guest profile or a secondary account. Using a guest profile ensures that custom control settings do not overwrite Player 1's primary configurations.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Shared Career Progression and Split-Screen Features">

One of the most significant upgrades in the sequel is how local co-op integrates with the main campaign. Instead of limiting secondary players to simple guest roles, local play allows both participants to make meaningful progress.

When playing in Career Mode, all unlocked jobs, earned Cash, and PowerWash Points are tied directly to the host's save file. However, both players can utilize the equipment unlocked by the host, ensuring that Player 2 is not left using weak starting tools while Player 1 operates professional-grade machinery.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Troubleshooting and FAQ">

While local split-screen is highly stable, players may occasionally run into controller detection issues, performance drops on older hardware, or UI scaling preferences. 

Using performance mode on consoles and ensuring both controllers are fully assigned to separate profiles before launching the game will resolve the vast majority of local multiplayer issues.

<Callout type="info" title="Performance Tip">
If you experience frame rate drops during split-screen play on the Nintendo Switch 2 or lower-end PCs, navigate to the graphics options and lower the water puddle reflection quality. This significantly improves performance without affecting gameplay.
</Callout>
